Indigenous Textiles & Weaving Traditions

This exhibit celebrates pre-colonial and regional textile arts, featuring:

  • 18th-century T'boli t'nalak weavings
  • Ilocos inabel cloth samples with geometric patterns
  • Visayan hablon fabric with natural dyes
  • Interactive loom demonstrations

These textiles preserve ancestral knowledge while showcasing vibrant color symbolism and weaving techniques passed through generations.

Modern Sculptures & Experimental Media

Our contemporary wing highlights boundary-pushing works like:

  • Fractured Horizons (bronze, 1978) by L. Cruz
  • Digital projection Memoryscapes (2022)
  • Recycled metal installation Urban Echoes
  • Interactive augmented reality sculpture Layers of Time

This collection bridges traditional craftsmanship with modern technology, inviting visitors to engage with art through multiple senses.
